I've decided Python to be the language that I'd use in this hypothetical scenario. Flake8 and Ruff seem to be compelling options for linting in the Python ecosystem. In terms of testing, PyTest seems to be the crowd favourite. Since Python is an interpreted language, there isn't a build step tool for normal Python deployment. In terms of building a Python package; Flit and Poetry seem decent options.
There are many alternatives to the industry favourites of Jenkins and Github Actions for CI. The following are what were listed in the first article I found on Google: Spacelift, GitLab CI, CircleCI, Travis CI, CodeShip, AWS CodePipeline, Azure DevOps, Bitbucket Pipelines and TeamCity.
The decision to choose a self-hosted or cloud-hosted CI environment is usually dictated by the resources required by the build process. Even with a small team (like this example of around 6 developers), the application being developed may depend on special resources (e.g. a GPU). If your application depends on special resources, you're better off going with a self-hosted CI solution. If your application doesn't depend on special resources; you're better off going with a cloud-based solution until your team grows in size where you may need a curated, self-hosted solution to meet your relatively large team's CI needs.
